Communities
 As you've might have seen in the word cloud on the previous page, not only tags were used in support of the American right-wing (Republicans), the 'left' (Democrats) were supported by these tweets as well. The database I used, discovered that there were actual seperate communities within these Russian trolls. What does it mean to be a community?

Well, these trolls didn't only tweet about certain topics, they interacted with each other as well. They did this through retweeting: if you retweet something, you copy another user's comment on the Twitter website for your own followers to read. Through retweeting, the trolls were reinforcing each others messages and strengthening the message they tried to disperse. By analyzing which users tweeted each other, I was able group certain trolls into communities!

Through this, we were able to determine that most of the original tweets written in the Russian troll network were written by a small number of users, such as @TEN_GOP. As mentioned above, the majority of overall tweets were retweets, because many of the Russian troll accounts were solely retweeting other accounts in an attempt to amplify the message.

So, what can social media platforms and governments do to monitor and prevent future abuse? First, it’s a matter of connections. In today’s hyper-connected world, it’s difficult to identify relationships in a dataset if you’re not using a technology purpose-built for storing connected data. It’s even more difficult if you’re not looking for connections in the first place.

Second, once something as this is realised, it is important for governments and tech-companies to take action to solve these issues. A democracy is led by the will of the people, but what if the will of the people isn't based on their own ration thoughts -- but rather on thoughts injected in their heads by external sources, e.g. through the media? What's left of a democracy at that point?

And last but not least: be critical. Think about what you read and don't take anything for granted you see online, because in this higly mediatized society we live in nowadays, not everything is really real, and every person can be fake.
