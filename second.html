The Ultimate Revelation Of Russian Trolls


#Tags
 I wondered, what did these trolls tweet about? The database I researched had an overview of all the actual tweets that were sent out by these Russian users, but reading them all would take a long time and making a subsequent conclusion would take even longer.

Thankfully, Twitter users regularly engage in the use of hashtags: a word or phrase preceded by a hash sign (#), used on social media websites and applications, especially Twitter, to identify messages on a specific topic. By looking at these tags, I was able to get a quick overview at the topics of these tweets, to see if they were actually tweeting about the elections. And they were.

Even more so, I couldn't even find a single tag that didn't contain anything related to something in the political agenda. The amount of tags about the presidential elections is quite literally, insane.

Take a look at the word cloud I placed at the bottom of this page. It contains all hashtags used, seperated by the month of the year (2016). The bigger a tag, the more frequently it was used.

The hashtags #Trump, #BlackLivesMatter, #Election2016 and #Brexit were four of the top 10 most used hashtags in 2016.

Before I started exploring the data base, I had this idea that Russian trolls had the aim of making Donald Trump president, so I expected all tags to support the Trump campaign, and to disapprove the campaign of his primary opponent, Hillary Clinton. Yet they didn't. Yeah, you see a lot of #Trump and #MakeAmericaGreatAgain, but that's not all. There are also a lot of tweets containing tags supporting #Clinton and the #BlackLivesMatter campaign. Why? Why would you support both sides of the spectrum? I was clueless.

And then it hit me. I saw a fascinating #video on YouTube, about an interview with an ex-KGB agent discussing Russian propaganda tactics. In short, he talks about how they (during the Soviet-era) tried to disrupt other nations by splitting them up in seperate groups and pushing both groups to their respective (ideological) extremes. "Divide and conquer", as some would say. They were creating seperate #communities, and pit them against each other. Interesting.

Take a look for yourself. See how these trolls tweeted over 2016, and take a specific look at how they tweeted in the months leading up to the Presidential elections. What do you notice?
